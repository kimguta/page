EATOF(East Asia Inter Regional Tourism Forum) is an international tourism association that was founded in the year 2000 by 9 local governments across East Asia under the initiative of Gangwon Province, Korea. The EATOF, which is the only tourism association in East Asia, have been making effort to enlarge local tourism industry and reinforce the bases for practical cooperation between member provinces. Currently, EATOF has 10 member province from 10 East Asia countries as Gangwon(Korea), Tottori(Japan), Jilin(China), Tuv(Mongolia), Cebu(Philippines), Sarawak(Malaysia), Yogyakarta(Indonesia), Quang Ninh(Vietnam), Luang Prabang(Laos) and Siem Reap(Cambodia).

Wat Xiengthong

This temple is not only the most beautiful in Luang Prabang, but arguably one of the most spectacular temples in the entire country. Built by King Setthatirath around 1560 it is a classic example of Luang Prabang style and features a gracefully sloping roof and glass [tile] murals. The best time to visit, especially for photography, is in the morning.

Kuang Si Waterfall

Kuangsi Waterfall is a 45-minute, 30 km drive south of Luang Prabang town. Legend has it that a hermit used a magic stick to dig for water, which sprang up and created this waterfall. A golden deer took up refuge under one of the rocks. The name of the fall is derived from this story as “Kuang” means deer and “Si” means to bore a hole. The cool stream flows down hundreds of meters over 6 cascades of limestone rocks with many pools perfect for swimming. During the rainy season, the volume of water flow increases immensely and causes erosion.

Ha Long Bay

Covering an area of 43,400 ha and including over 1600 islands and islets, most of which are uninhabitated and unaffected by humans, it forms a spectacular seascape of limestone pillars and is an ideal model of a mature Karst landscape developed during a warm and wet tropical climate. The property’s exceptional scenic beauty is complemented by its great biological interest.
The outstanding value of the property is centered around the drowned limestone karst landforms, displaying spectacular pillars with a variety of coastal erosional features such as arches and caves which form a majestic natural scenery.

Yen Tu

The Yen Tu historic and scenic area has an area of ​​about 9,295 hectares, including many religious architectural works of pagodas, temples and tower stupas built since the Ly Dynasty (1009-1225). The dense relic system, amid a majestic natural area, including dozens of pagodas and temples, hundreds of towers, thousands of rare and precious antiques, especially rare scriptures and books, preserves spiritual and ideological values of Truc Lam Zen Buddhism and the splendid and brilliant culture of Dai Viet civilization.
The beauty of Yen Tu lies in the magnificence of mountains, the solemnity of pagodas and towers, the poetry of landscapes, and the diversity of plants.

The city of Miri serves as a gateway to dive destinations. The Miri-Sibuti Coral Reefs National Park is a protected coral reef area and the largest offshore national park established. Over 30 untouched coral reefs can be found and it contains almost 40 different diving sites that are well-known.
The coral reefs provide some of the most diverse and easily accessible aquatic species in the area.

Angkor Wat​ is the well-known ancient temple site in Cambodia. With its 5 lotus-like towers rising 65 meters into the sky, it is an awe-inspiring sight. This UNESCO World Heritage site was once the largest pre-industrial city in the world and is the most famous tourist attraction in Cambodia. It has become the symbol of Cambodia and the pride of its people. The massive temple site is a must-see when visiting the country.

Kompong Phluk Floating Village

Kompong Phluk Floating Village, located 20 km in the Southeast of Siem Reap City, is a permanent floating village with wooden houses built on stilts up to 10 m high to avoid flooding during rainy season. The villagers make a living by fishing and primary in shrimp harvesting. Additionally, they work in the tourism sector to support community by rowing mini boat and guiding travelers to see flooded mangrove forest surrounds the area which is home to a variety of wildlife including crab-eating macaques.

The Tottori Sand Dune

The Tottori Sand Dune is a must for any visitor to Tottori. Located in the heart of the San’in Kaigan Geopark and stretching 2.4km north to south and 16km east to west, it is one of the finest coastal sand dunes in the country. The ripples swept in the sand by the winds of the Sea of Japan are a constant delight to visitors. The small hill known as Umanose (‘the horse’s back’) offers a picturesque panorama of the ocean.

Mt Daisen

The marvelous scenery at Japan’s third national park continues to capture the hearts of visitors. At an elevation of 1,709 metres, Mt Daisen’s peak, called Misen, is the highest in the Chuugoku region. Green from spring through summer, red in autumn, and covered with snow in winter, the area captivates through the seasons.

Uradome Beach (San’in Kaigan Geopark)

Within the San’in Kaigan Geopark is the Uradome Shore, a natural museum of geology and terrain where the raging waves of the Sea of Japan carved tunnels and caves and formed beaches of white sand. From Mt Shichiyama to the Kugami Cape, the picturesque seaside changes appearance with the time of day in a way that had the literary master Touson Shimazaki singing its praises. The characteristic Uradome scenery in spots like the Natanego and Senganmatsu islands, Kamoga rocky beach, and Ryujin hollow never fail to captivate.

Manzushir Monastry

Manzushir Monastry located in the middle of a larch forest at an altitude of 1800 meters above sea level. The foundation of Manzushir Monastery was laid when the first temple of Manzushir Monastery was built on the slopes of Bogdhan Mountain in 1733 under the leadership of Luvsanjambaldanzan, the first incarnation of Manzushir Monk.
Today, the ruins of the monastery and the images of big deities carved on the rocks of the mountain slopes remain as a rare and precious historical and cultural monument, which was protected by the state in 1998.

Chinggis Khaan Statue Complex

"Tsonjin Boldog" is a historical place and statue located in Erdene Soum of the Tuv Province. The whole statue is 48 meters high, including the central base structure. The horse tail is about 10 meters high and 30 meters in diameter, and the equestrian statue is 38 meters high.
The statue of Chinggis Khan with a whip is made of 250 tons of steels which makes the largest equestrian statue in the world. Inside the building, there are attractions and services such as an art gallery, a museum with artifacts from the Hunnu/ Xiongnu period, a restaurant, a conference hall, a meeting room, a billiards room, and a gift shop etc.

PRAMBANAN TEMPLE

The temple compound, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest Hindu temple site in Indonesia and the second-largest in Southeast Asia after Angkor Wat. It is characterized by its tall and pointed architecture, typical of Hindu architecture, and by the towering 47-metre-high (154 ft) central building inside a large complex of individual temples. Prambanan temple compounds originally consists of 240 temple structures; which represents the grandeur of ancient Java's Hindu art and architecture, also considered as a masterpiece of the classical period in Indonesia. Prambanan attracts many visitors from around the world.

KRATON NGAYOGYAKARTA HADININGRAT

The Kraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat is a palace complex in the city of Yogyakarta Special Region, Indonesia. It is the seat of the reigning Sultan of Yogyakarta and his family. The complex is a center of Javanese culture, and contains a museum displaying royal artifacts. It is guarded by the Yogyakarta Kraton Guards . The complex was built in 1755–1756 for Hamengkubuwono I, the first Sultan of Yogyakarta It was one of the monarch's first acts after the signing of the Treaty of Giyanti, which recognized the creation of the Sultanate of Yogyakarta under the Dutch East India Company. A banyan forest, protected from flooding by its location between two rivers, was chosen as the site for the palace.
